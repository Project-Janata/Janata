Janata — the journey from vouch to advocate

How a CHYK who just moved cities goes from a friend's text to a member who brings others in. The aha is belonging (joining a home center), sparked by an invite, made concrete by RSVPing to real events.

The core tension: vet the community (accounts are invite-only) while it spreads organically (anyone 18+ can RSVP; members mint invites). The invite is both the gate and the growth engine.

Takeaways (committed)

  • RSVP before account. The emotion peak (RSVP) must stay wall-free — account-less RSVP keeps the top of funnel wide while membership stays invite-gated. Payoff precedes effort.
  • The invite is the product's growth engine, not just a gate. Design "invite a friend" as a first-class, rewarding member action (post-RSVP nudge, share an event).
  • Vet + spread reconciled: events spread to anyone (account-less, email-captured, auto-linked on join); accounts stay curated (invite-only). Two funnels, one loop.
  • Ask 18+ once (profile, or at RSVP if no profile). No public feed shown to logged-out users anywhere.

Ideas (uncommitted sparks)

  • Post-RSVP invite: "Bring someone — invite a friend to this event." Turns the RSVP peak straight into a share.
  • Social proof on cards: "3 from your center are going" + faces, to pull the browse stage upward.
  • Invite as gift: a member gets a small batch of invites to "give"; scarcity makes them feel valuable and intentional (keeps vetting real).
  • Manage RSVP by email (magic link) for account-less attendees — edit/cancel without an account.
  • Center welcome: when Priya joins, a board post auto-introduces her so the belonging beat is mutual.
  • Seva/points for showing up + bringing people — engagement + spread, if it doesn't cheapen the tone.
